"Doctor, how much does an implant cost?" It's a question I hear at least 3 times a day. And the honest answer is: it depends. But I'm not saying that to dodge the question — it's because the price of a dental implant genuinely varies based on several factors that I'll walk you through below.

## Why Does the Price of a Dental Implant Vary?

If you've searched "dental implant Satu Mare price" on Google, you've probably seen figures ranging from 2,000 to 7,000 lei (roughly 400 to 1,400 EUR). That's a big gap, I know. But it's not random.

The price comes down to three things:

**1. The implant brand.** There are Korean, Israeli, German, and Swiss implants. A Straumann implant (Switzerland) is priced differently than a MegaGen or Osstem. That doesn't mean the more affordable ones are bad — but differences in materials, clinical studies, and warranty do exist.

**2. The crown on the implant.** Many patients don't realize that the implant price and the crown price are two separate things. An implant without a crown is like a foundation without a house. The crown can be monolithic zirconia, layered ceramic, or porcelain-fused-to-metal — each at a different price point.

**3. Additional procedures.** Sometimes the bone isn't thick enough and a bone graft (augmentation) is needed. Other times the gum tissue needs to be corrected. These procedures add to the total cost.

## Cheap vs. Expensive Dental Implant — Is the Difference Worth It?

Honestly? Sometimes yes, sometimes no.

I've worked with multiple systems over my 10 years of experience and I can tell you the main difference comes down to predictability. Premium implants have clinical studies spanning 20-30 years that demonstrate their success rate. More affordable ones have studies covering 5-10 years — which still show good results, but with less long-term history.

My recommendation: don't choose based on price alone. Ask your dentist which system they use and why. An implant placed correctly, in the right bone, with proper hygiene afterward — it lasts a lifetime, regardless of brand.

But an implant placed incorrectly, even the most expensive one, can fail.

## How Long Does the Dental Implant Procedure Take?

This is the second question I hear every day. And the answer surprises many people: the procedure itself — meaning the insertion of the implant into the bone — takes about 30-45 minutes per implant.

It doesn't hurt. It's done under local anesthesia, the same kind we use for a filling. Many patients tell me afterward that they expected it to be much worse.

What takes longer is the healing. The bone needs 3-6 months to fuse with the implant (a process called osseointegration). During this period, you wear a temporary restoration — so you're never walking around without a tooth.

The complete timeline looks something like this:

1. **Day 1:** Consultation + 3D X-ray + treatment plan
2. **Surgery day:** Implant placement (30-45 min)
3. **Month 1-2:** Periodic check-ups, gum healing
4. **Month 3-6:** Osseointegration (the implant integrates with the bone)
5. **After healing:** Digital impression + placement of the final crown

Total? About 4-6 months from placement to your final smile. But throughout this entire time, you have a temporary tooth and go about your life normally.

## All-on-4 and All-on-6 — When Are They Worth It?

If you've been told you need multiple implants — let's say 6 or more on one arch — there's a more efficient and more affordable alternative than individual implants.

It's called All-on-4 (or All-on-6) and it works like this: 4-6 implants are strategically placed in the jaw, and a complete, fixed prosthesis is attached to them. The result? Fixed teeth in 24 hours, in some cases even the same day.

The major advantage: the total cost is lower than if you were to place 8-10 individual implants with separate crowns. And the treatment time is significantly shorter.

The price for All-on-4 starts at approximately 15,000 lei (approx. 3,000 EUR) per arch, but varies depending on the materials chosen and the complexity of the case.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Dental Implants

**How much does a dental implant hurt?**
Less than you'd expect. The procedure is done under local anesthesia and takes under an hour. Afterward, you may have mild discomfort for 2-3 days, manageable with ibuprofen. Most patients say it was easier than an extraction.

**How long does a dental implant last?**
With proper hygiene and annual check-ups — a lifetime. Implants have a success rate of over 95% long-term. The crown on the implant may need replacing after 15-20 years, but the implant itself stays.

**Can an implant be placed immediately after extraction?**
In some cases, yes. It's called an "immediate post-extraction implant" and it shortens the overall treatment time. But it doesn't apply in every situation — it depends on the condition of the bone and the location. We discuss this during the consultation.

**Is a dental implant covered by insurance?**
In Romania, dental implants are not covered by the national health insurance system. Some private insurance plans may partially cover the cost — check with your provider. We also offer installment payment options.

**At what age can you get implants?**
From age 18 and up, once the bone has finished growing. There's no upper age limit — I've successfully placed implants in patients aged 75 and older. What matters is your overall health, not your age.
